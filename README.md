I have a thing for low-level systems. Networking internals, distributed systems, systems programming. Most people treat these as separate areas. For me they're one continuous thread. How..!?

Imagine you have a perfectly working Distributed System (with a few Quintillion users). What's a Distributed System..? : /

A Bunch of Services(microservices) talking to each other. How do they talk..?

Over a network, Something like Kafka or RabbitMQ. What are those?

Networks. Communication. How does that communication actually work?

TCP, UDP, IP. How are those implemented?

C. What's the modern evolution of C?

Rust :)

(which is why my stack is Go, Rust, and occasionally Python.)

Linus said, 'Talk is cheap.' So here is my code... I mean projects.
